IP Addresses are what gives networks a way to help identify a specific computer or server. 
DNS helps in that by giving names to those users to help identify them for the IP Addreses.

Cookies are tiny bits of data that identify your computer when you use a certain network. A website can store it in a web browser so it can identify the user if they visit the site again. 

A URL's essential parts are the scheme (https), the subdomain (also the top and second level domains), and the subdirectory.
